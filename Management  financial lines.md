## Key portfolio message  
- Portfolio growth is modest, but reported profitability has surged sharply.  
**GWP** increased from **961** to **983** (+22, +2.3%), while UW result rose from **111** to **470** (+**359**, +**323**.4%).  
- The improvement is being driven much more by earning / retention / reserve movements than by top-line growth.  
**NEP** increased from **362** to **449** (+87, +24.0%), far ahead of **GWP** growth, helped by:  
o	higher retention: **NWP**/**GWP** improved from 41.1% to 45.5% (+4.4 pp)  
o	lower non-prop ceding: **109** to 76 (-33, -30.3%)  
o	higher CY release: **266** to **320** (+54, +20.3%)  
- Claims experience is exceptionally favorable on reported numbers, but heavily reserve-dependent.  
**NIC** after **IBNR** moved from +**123** to -**115** (improvement of **238**) and **IBNR** moved from +78 to -**111** (improvement of **189**).  
- Expense discipline improved at portfolio level.  
Expense reduced from **120** to **110** (-10, -8.3%), and Expense Ratio improved from 30% to 25% (-5 pp).  
- Acquisition cost increased only marginally versus premium growth.  
Acq Cost rose from 97 to **102** (+5, +5.2%), while Acq Cost % stayed flat at 10%.  
- RI economics improved.  
RI Commission increased from 88 to **117** (+29, +33.0%), supporting reported underwriting result.  
- The largest source of reported recovery is Mumbai and Corporate.  
o	Mumbai UW result: 39 to **256** (+**217**)  
o	Corporate UW result: **123** to **396** (+**273**)  
- Growth quality is mixed.  
Good-quality growth is visible in Bangalore, Delhi, and Retail, but a large part of total improvement still appears to come from reserve release / lower claim booking.  
- Portfolio concentration remains very high.  
Mumbai contributes **606** of **983** **GWP** (61.6%), and Corporate contributes **829** of **983** **GWP** (84.3%).  
- Management message: **FY26** looks materially stronger on reported metrics, but before scaling aggressively, management should validate whether the improvement is structural underwriting quality or reserve-led earnings.

## Immediate actions  
Underwriting actions  
- Validate **FY26** reserve release by zone and vertical before treating current margins as normalized earning power.  
- Re-segment Mumbai and Corporate into:   
o	structurally profitable accounts,  
o	reserve-release-led accounts,  
o	pricing remediation candidates.  
- Correct Chennai immediately:   
o	expense leakage review,  
o	minimum premium / deductible review,  
o	tighter referral thresholds.  
- Maintain underwriting discipline in Bangalore and Delhi so growth does not dilute **FY27** margins.  
- Protect Hyderabad quality; no need to force top-line growth at the expense of a clean low-loss book.  
RM / Distribution actions  
- Push growth hardest in Bangalore and Delhi, where premium and profit both improved.  
- Use Retail as a priority expansion pocket, but retain cost discipline given 20% Acq Cost %.  
- Stop volume-first behavior in **SME** Broking until margin quality improves.  
- Recover Ahmedabad scale selectively through renewal win-back / broker reactivation, but only where cost ratios are acceptable.  
- Prioritize renewals and cross-sell in Mumbai / Hyderabad / Delhi rather than indiscriminate new business.  
Portfolio management actions  
- Track reserve-release-adjusted **COR** / LR monthly.  
- Set separate appetite thresholds for:   
o	large Corporate accounts,  
o	**SME** Broking,  
o	cost-heavy low-scale zones.  
- Review reinsurance strategy because lower non-prop ceding materially helped net margin; ensure **FY26** benefit is sustainable.  
Data / **MIS** actions

- Split accident-year vs prior-year reserve movement.
