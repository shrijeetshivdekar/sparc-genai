# Product

## Register

product

## Users

ICICI Lombard relationship managers (RMs), sitting across a table from a startup founder during a sales meeting. The RM has 20-40 minutes. They need to look like the smartest person in the room on insurance, close the meeting with a signed bundle, and leave with a pricing sheet the founder can show their CFO.

Secondary: startup founders who self-serve the tool between RM visits, validating the risk report before signing.

## Product Purpose

SPARC analyzes a startup's risk profile across 13 regulatory and actuarial categories, then recommends the exact ICICI Lombard insurance bundle and individual products for that startup's sector, stage, and operating model. The output is a defensible, citation-backed risk report an RM can use as a sales artifact.

Success: the RM walks out of every meeting with a recommended bundle, a pricing estimate, and a startup that understands why they need it.

## Brand Personality

Intelligent. Clinical. Confident.

The tool never hedges. It cites research. It shows the math. It speaks with the authority of something that has read every IRDAI circular, every RBI enforcement action, every Swiss Re sigma report, and done the arithmetic for this specific startup.

## Anti-references

- Generic SaaS admin dashboards: Tailwind Admin / shadcn dashboard templates. Teal/blue accent, sidebar nav with icons, identical card grids, hero-metric strips everywhere, "Overview" headings, empty-state illustrations. This is the exact aesthetic to reject.
- Consumer fintech apps: Gradient blobs, bright primary colors, rounded everything, feels like a phone app store.
- Legacy insurance portals: Dense form tables, blue corporate, 2008 banking software energy.

## Design Principles

1. **Show the math.** Every risk score, multiplier, and recommendation must be traceable to a specific data point, regulation, or actuarial source. The RM can never be caught unable to answer "why this number?"

2. **Trust through specificity.** Indian laws, Indian loss statistics, named research papers, specific rupee figures. Generic global statements erode credibility. The more specific, the more trustworthy.

3. **Depth behind disclosure.** All the data is available, but layered. The top surface is scannable in 30 seconds. Full methodology is one click away, not always on screen.

4. **Speed to credibility.** The RM must look expert within 2 minutes of opening the tool with a client. Every default state should communicate authority, not loading spinners and empty states.

5. **Nothing generic.** Every output is for THIS startup, not any startup. Sector-specific language, named regulatory triggers, actual competitor examples. Generic outputs are indistinguishable from a chatbot.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum. B2B tool used on laptops in meeting rooms; standard desktop a11y requirements apply. Color is never the only signal for risk severity (always paired with labels and icons). Respect prefers-reduced-motion.
