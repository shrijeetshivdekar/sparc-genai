SPARC / Runtime modes
Designed for safe rollout
Mode 01
`off`
Serve deterministic recommendations only. GenAI metadata stays explicit, but recommendation ranking does not change.
Mode 02
`shadow`
Run Gemini in parallel, compare its ranking to deterministic output, log the diff, but still serve the deterministic result to the user.
Mode 03
`primary`
Serve the GenAI-ranked shortlist only after deterministic guardrails and validation checks pass.
Guardrails that stay deterministic
A
Hard gates
The model cannot recommend a product or bundle that deterministic eligibility has rejected.
B
Compliance triggers
Regulatory and underwriting triggers are computed before reranking and remain enforceable regardless of model output.
C
Fallbacks
If Gemini is unavailable, slow, malformed, or contradictory, SPARC falls back cleanly to deterministic recommendations.
Why this matters internally: `shadow` mode gives Commercial Lines a way to observe how often Gemini would have changed the ranking before choosing to trust it in `primary`. That is a safer rollout pattern than a direct switch to AI-first recommendations.
SPARC can move from observation to production without removing deterministic safety rails.
