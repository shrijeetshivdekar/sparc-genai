It analyses the startup's risk across 13 dimensions. It selects the single best ICICI Lombard product for them. It explains exactly why that product fits. It estimates the premium range. And it writes a warm, ready-to-send personalised email — all in under two minutes.

The RM walks into every meeting fully armed. Let me show you this live."

The Full Risk Score Formula
Step 1 — Base score (out of 10) from Sector Profile
Every sector has a hardcoded base score for each of the 13 risk categories. Example for Fintech:

Risk Category	Base Score
Cyber Technical	10
Data Privacy	10
Liability	9
Governance & Fraud	9
Regulatory Compliance	10
Geopolitical	8
Reputation	9
Key Person	8
Policy Velocity	9
IP Infringement	5
Property	3
ESG & Climate	3
Gig & Labour	5
If a sub-sector is provided (e.g. Fintech.PA_PG), those base scores are overridden with sub-sector specific values.

Step 2 — Multipliers applied per category
Each risk category gets multiplied by a different combination of these factors:

Stage Multiplier (default — most categories)

Pre-seed → ×0.70
Seed     → ×0.90
Series A → ×1.10
Series B+→ ×1.30
Exceptions:

Governance & Fraud has its own U-shaped multiplier: Pre-seed ×0.90 · Seed ×0.85 · Series A ×1.00 · Series B+ ×1.30
Policy Velocity has an inverse multiplier: Pre-seed ×1.10 · Seed ×1.00 · Series A ×0.95 · Series B+ ×1.00
Team Size Multiplier
Team Size	General (team_mult)	Key Person (kp_mult)
≤ 5	×0.80	×1.50
6–10	×1.00	×1.40
11–20	×1.00	×1.20
21–50	×1.15	×1.00
51–150	×1.30	×0.85
150+	×1.50	×0.70
Operations Multiplier
Digital-only	Physical-only	Hybrid
Property	×0.4	×1.4	×1.0
Liability	×0.8	×1.2	×1.0
Cyber	×1.2	×0.7	×1.0
Gig & Labour	×0.6	×1.3	×1.0
Data Sensitivity Multiplier
Low	Medium	High
Cyber Technical	×0.6	×1.0	×1.4
Data Privacy	×0.5	×1.0	×1.5
Regulatory Compliance	×0.8	×1.0	×1.3
Dynamic Adjusters (from profile inputs)
Adjuster	Formula	Affects
SDF probability	1.0 + (sdf_prob × 0.5)	Data Privacy
Chinese investor / supplier	1.0 + (cn_investor% × 0.8) + (cn_supplier% × 0.4)	Geopolitical
Gig headcount %	1.0 + (gig% × 1.2) — capped at ×2.0	Gig & Labour
Gig state risk (KA/RJ/BR/JH/TG)	×1.25 bonus if gig% > 10%	Gig & Labour
Hardware split %	1.0 + (hw_split × 0.6)	Property, Regulatory
EU export %	1.0 + (eu_pct × 1.5)	ESG & Climate, Geopolitical
AI in product	×1.3	Regulatory Compliance, Policy Velocity
Founder concentration	1.0 + (concentration × 0.5) + (0.3 if raised > ₹2,000cr)	Governance & Fraud
BRSR dependency	×1.2	ESG & Climate
Climate risk zone	Low ×1.0 / Medium ×1.2 / High ×1.5 / Extreme ×1.8	ESG & Climate, Property
B2B revenue %	1.0 + (b2b% × 0.4)	Liability
Liability-team factor	0.85 + 0.08 × ln(team_size / 10)	Liability, Reputation
Step 3 — Final score formula per category

Raw = base_score × [applicable multipliers]
Score (0–100) = Raw × 7.5, capped at 100
The × 7.5 converts the base-10 scale to 0–100. A sector base score of 10 with a stage multiplier of 1.30 and data multiplier of 1.5 would give 10 × 1.30 × 1.50 × 7.5 = 146.25 → capped at 100.

Step 4 — Overall score

Overall = average of all 13 category scores
That's the single number shown at the top (e.g. Battery Smart's 65.4/100).
