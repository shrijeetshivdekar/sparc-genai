Why this matters for PayNova — specific risk drivers
RBI's PA Framework (March 2022) requires all payment aggregators to hold cyber insurance with minimum coverage of ₹5 Cr. PayNova's PA licence makes this non-negotiable.
DPDP Act §10 exposure: handling KYC / Aadhaar data at Series A scale qualifies PayNova as a "Significant Data Fiduciary" if processing exceeds 10 lakh individuals. SDF designation probability: 78%.
Sequoia and Accel typically mandate D&O as a Series A board governance condition. Without it, the next fundraise is likely to face a compliance gap flag in due diligence.
Peer loss data: Razorpay (Series D analogue) reported ₹3.2 Cr in cyber-incident-related costs in 2023; Cashfree settled an RBI enforcement action for ₹1.5 Cr in Q3 2024. These are direct comparables for a ₹200 Cr/month PA operator.

Regulatory triggers — immediate action required

RBI PA Framework — Cyber Insurance Obligation
Payment Aggregator licence holders must maintain adequate cyber risk insurance under RBI circular RBI/2021-22/60 (March 2022). Failure to comply can result in licence suspension proceedings. This is non-negotiable and time-sensitive.
RBI/2021-22/60 · DPSS.CO.PD.No.757/02.14.003/2021-22 · Effective 31 March 2023

DPDP Act §10 — Significant Data Fiduciary Designation Risk
PayNova handles KYC / Aadhaar / financial transaction data. SDF designation probability: 78%. SDFs face enhanced obligations under DPDP Act including mandatory DPIA, annual audit, and DPO appointment. SDF-specific insurance riders are available and should be added to the cyber policy.
