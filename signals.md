# Signals

## 30 Public Business Signals Matrix

This matrix tracks observable public events or operating pivots that can change a startup's risk profile. These are not generic funding milestones. They are business, regulatory, physical, cyber, people, and transit signals that an RM or underwriting workflow can monitor and map to ICICI Lombard commercial insurance products.

| Risk Category | Signal | Why It Matters | Likely Insurance Angle |
|---|---|---|---|
| Liability | Integration as a seller or buyer node on the ONDC network | Subjects the startup to ONDC Issue and Grievance Management (IGM) and Online Dispute Resolution (ODR), creating multi-party accountability for product defects across a decentralized network. | ICICI Lombard Commercial General Liability (CGL) and Product Liability for third-party indemnifications arising from binding ODR settlements. |
| Liability | Launching digital educational or interactive gaming modules targeting users under 18 | Triggers the highest protection tier of DPDP Rules 2025. Processing children's data without verifiable parental consent can create severe statutory penalty exposure, including penalties up to INR 200 crore per instance. | Cyber Liability with regulatory fines sub-limits, plus Directors and Officers (D&O) Liability for statutory defense costs. |
| Liability | Publicly updating Terms of Service to include automated third-party AI or LLM decision-making | Use of large language models for customer advisory introduces algorithmic bias and hallucination risks, which can lead to professional negligence claims if AI gives materially incorrect operational advice. | Errors and Omissions (E&O) / Professional Indemnity for technology services and automated advisory failures. |
| Liability | Executing sublease agreements to monetize excess commercial real estate to unaffiliated micro-entities | Converts the startup from a standard commercial tenant into a de facto commercial landlord, creating premises liability for bodily injury, fire spread, and damage caused by unvetted sub-tenants. | Commercial General Liability (CGL) with endorsements for sub-tenancy and extended premises liability. |
| Liability | Appointing an external high-profile Data Protection Officer and announcing a compliance overhaul | Publicly indicates a transition toward Significant Data Fiduciary obligations under the DPDP Act. The DPO mandate may expose historical data architecture breaches requiring regulatory response. | D&O Liability for the DPO and board, plus Cyber Liability. |
| Liability | Pivoting from software-only SaaS to distributing white-labeled consumer hardware | Moves the primary exposure from intangible software and data risk to bodily injury, property damage, product defect, and strict liability risks. | Product Liability, transitioning the account away from a purely technology E&O exposure profile. |
| Cyber and Financial Lines | Initiating registration with the DPBI to operate as an authorized Consent Manager | Enters a regulated intermediary class requiring net worth, audits, interoperability, and high uptime. Downtime or security failure can create multi-party data exposure. | Cyber Liability with higher capacity, plus E&O for systemic platform failures. |
| Cyber and Financial Lines | Deploying checkout finance or BNPL through a regulated NBFC partner | Can classify the startup as a Lending Service Provider under RBI digital lending guidelines. UI, consent, disclosure, or Key Fact Statement failures can compromise partner NBFC compliance. | E&O / Technology Professional Liability for non-compliant financial infrastructure. |
| Cyber and Financial Lines | Publishing and open-sourcing proprietary internal API infrastructure to developer communities | Expands digital attack surface. Vulnerabilities in open-source code can enable data exfiltration, DPDP violation notices, and reputational damage. | Cyber Liability for third-party breach response, forensics, and notification expenses. |
| Cyber and Financial Lines | Migrating enterprise architecture from centralized on-premise servers to multi-cloud federated environments | Data migration creates peak vulnerability. Misconfigured cloud access permissions are a common vector for major data breaches and mandatory breach reporting. | Cyber Liability with underwriting review of cloud security posture and incident response readiness. |
| Cyber and Financial Lines | Mandating facial recognition or advanced biometric onboarding for application access | Escalates the entity into highly sensitive personal data processing. Biometric breach creates irreversible privacy harm and higher regulatory sensitivity. | Cyber Liability with policy language addressing biometric data exposure and statutory penalty response. |
| Cyber and Financial Lines | Announcing integration with TReDS platforms to discount MSME invoices as a supplier | Indicates working-capital pressure and receivables monetization. Exposes the business to buyer default, counter-party failure, and factoring-chain risk. | Trade Credit Insurance against non-payment, insolvency, and protracted default. |
| Property and Engineering | Retrofitting offices with captive lithium-ion battery banks for UPS or power backup | Creates severe localized fire risk. Lithium-ion thermal runaway is difficult to suppress with standard office sprinkler systems and can endanger the entire building. | Fire and Burglary / SME Package with engineering warranties for Battery Management Systems (BMS). |
| Property and Engineering | Installing heavy liquid-immersion cooling infrastructure in standard server rooms | Signals major compute-density scale-up, often for AI. Introduces liquid damage and short-circuit risks to high-value electronic equipment. | Electronic Equipment Insurance (EEI) and Commercial Property with bespoke coverage for leakage and short circuits. |
| Property and Engineering | Commissioning high-voltage captive solar rooftops on leased operational assets | Alters structural load and introduces installation-phase construction risk. Faulty high-voltage wiring can drive industrial fire exposure. | Erection All Risks (EAR) during installation, converting to Fire and Perils after commissioning. |
| Property and Engineering | Procuring automated robotic sorting arms for micro-fulfillment centers | High-capex machinery creates breakdown risk. Failure can halt fulfillment operations immediately and trigger business interruption losses. | Machinery Breakdown and Contractor's Plant and Machinery (CPM). |
| Property and Engineering | Leasing a recognized heritage building for a flagship retail experience center | Heritage properties may lack modern fire compartmentalization, sprinkler systems, and structural resilience, creating asymmetric property risk. | Commercial Property Insurance with higher underwriting scrutiny and premium loading. |
| Property and Engineering | Implementing dense server-rack deployments in standard office spaces without industrial HVAC | High-density compute clusters in non-data-center environments can overheat, increasing electrical fire and cascading hardware failure risk. | Fire and Perils plus Electronic Equipment Insurance (EEI). |
| Employee Benefits | Mandating a non-negotiable five-day return-to-office policy after remote work | Abrupt work-condition changes can trigger resignations, mental health claims, and constructive dismissal or discriminatory enforcement allegations. | D&O with Employment Practices Liability (EPLI) extensions, plus Group Health Insurance. |
| Employee Benefits | Registering gig workers under the Karnataka Platform-Based Gig Workers Welfare Board | Publicly acknowledges aggregator status and welfare obligations. Triggers welfare fees, termination protocols, and state-monitored grievance mechanisms. | Workmen's Compensation and Group Personal Accident (GPA). |
| Employee Benefits | Deploying field sales or logistics teams to high-altitude or severe-climate Tier-3 regions | Shifts people risk from controlled office environments to geographies with road, disease, climate, and medical access risks. | Group Personal Accident and Group Health Insurance with geographic coverage endorsements. |
| Employee Benefits | Announcing workforce restructuring or downsizing above 15 percent of headcount | Mass layoffs can lead to wrongful termination, discrimination, contract violation, and board-level litigation. | D&O Liability with robust EPLI extensions. |
| Employee Benefits | Introducing mandatory extreme outdoor activities for team-building events | Company-sponsored events outside normal office hazards introduce severe bodily injury and fatality risk. | Group Personal Accident and Workmen's Compensation. |
| Employee Benefits | Moving sales teams to heavily commissioned performance-only compensation | High-pressure compensation models can increase workplace stress, toxic culture claims, unethical sales conduct, and regulatory scrutiny. | D&O with EPLI, plus E&O for mis-selling risks. |
| Transit and Motor | Replacing owned last-mile delivery fleet with second-hand refurbished electric vehicles | Second-hand EV batteries may have degraded cells, increasing thermal runaway risk during charging or collision and endangering drivers, goods, and public infrastructure. | Commercial Motor Fleet with battery-age underwriting, BIS AIS-156 compliance review, and fire-exclusion scrutiny. |
| Transit and Motor | Moving from centralized Tier-1 logistics provider to fragmented regional 3PL network | Reduces chain-of-custody visibility and standardization, increasing cargo theft, mishandling, delay, and safety protocol gaps. | Marine Transit / Inland Transit with optimized retentions and higher deductibles. |
| Transit and Motor | Extending B2B credit payment terms from 30 days to 90 days for distributor networks | Can signal working-capital strain and increases counter-party default exposure over a longer credit window. | Trade Credit Insurance for distributor insolvency or protracted default. |
| Transit and Motor | Deploying customized mobile experience vans for localized experiential marketing | Combines commercial motor exposure with public footfall and event-like liability around moving assets. | Commercial Motor Fleet and Commercial General Liability (CGL). |
| Transit and Motor | Piloting autonomous floor-cleaning robots in partner retail environments | Autonomous machinery in public spaces creates third-party injury and property damage risk from sensor, navigation, or control failure. | Commercial General Liability (CGL) and Product Liability. |
| Transit and Motor | Implementing a hyper-aggressive 10-minute delivery SLA for local perishables | Aggressive SLAs can pressure gig riders into traffic violations, route shortcuts, and unsafe behavior, increasing road accident and third-party damage frequency. | Commercial Motor Fleet and Group Personal Accident for rider networks. |
